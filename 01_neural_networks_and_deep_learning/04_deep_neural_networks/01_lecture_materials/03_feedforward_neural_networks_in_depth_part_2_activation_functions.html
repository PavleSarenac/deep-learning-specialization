Feedforward Neural Networks in Depth, Part 2: Activation Functions

Dec 21, 2021

This is the second post of a three-part series in which we derive the mathematics behind feedforward neural networks. We worked our way through forward and backward propagations in the first post, but if you remember, we only mentioned activation functions in passing. In particular, we did not derive an analytic expression for ∂aj,i[l]/∂zj,i[l] or, by extension, ∂J/∂zj,i[l]. So let us pick up the derivations where we left off.

ReLU

The rectified linear unit, or ReLU for short, is given by

aj,i[l]=gj[l](z1,i[l],…,zj,i[l],…,zn[l],i[l])=max(0,zj,i[l])={zj,i[l]if zj,i[l]>0,0otherwise.

In other words,

(1)A[l]=max(0,Z[l]).

Next, we compute the partial derivatives of the activations in the current layer:

∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]:={1if zj,i[l]>0,0otherwise,=I(zj,i[l]>0),∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=0,∀p≠j.

It follows that

∂J∂zj,i[l]=∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]+∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]I(zj,i[l]>0),

which we can vectorize as

(2)∂J∂Z[l]=∂J∂A[l]⊙I(Z[l]>0),

where ⊙ denotes element-wise multiplication.

Sigmoid

The sigmoid activation function is given by

aj,i[l]=gj[l](z1,i[l],…,zj,i[l],…,zn[l],i[l])=σ(zj,i[l])=11+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]).

Vectorization yields

(3)A[l]=11+exp⁡(−Z[l]).

To practice backward propagation, first, we construct a computation graph:

u0=zj,i[l],u1=−u0,u2=exp⁡(u1),u3=1+u2,u4=1u3=aj,i[l].

Then, we perform an outside first traversal of the chain rule:

∂aj,i[l]∂u4=1,∂aj,i[l]∂u3=∂aj,i[l]∂u4∂u4∂u3=−1u32=−1(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u2=∂aj,i[l]∂u3∂u3∂u2=−1u32=−1(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u1=∂aj,i[l]∂u2∂u2∂u1=−1u32exp⁡(u1)=−exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u0=∂aj,i[l]∂u1∂u1∂u0=1u32exp⁡(u1)=exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2.

Let us simplify:

∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])−1(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=11+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])−1(1+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l]).

We also note that

∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=0,∀p≠j.

Consequently,

∂J∂zj,i[l]=∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]+∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l]).

Lastly, no summations mean trivial vectorization:

(4)∂J∂Z[l]=∂J∂A[l]⊙A[l]⊙(1−A[l]).

Tanh

The hyperbolic tangent function, i.e., the tanh activation function, is given by

aj,i[l]=gj[l](z1,i[l],…,zj,i[l],…,zn[l],i[l])=tanh⁡(zj,i[l])=exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l])exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]).

By utilizing element-wise multiplication, we get

(5)A[l]=1exp⁡(Z[l])+exp⁡(−Z[l])⊙(exp⁡(Z[l])−exp⁡(−Z[l])).

Once again, let us introduce intermediate variables to practice backward propagation:

u0=zj,i[l],u1=−u0,u2=exp⁡(u0),u3=exp⁡(u1),u4=u2−u3,u5=u2+u3,u6=1u5,u7=u4u6=aj,i[l].

Next, we compute the partial derivatives:

∂aj,i[l]∂u7=1,∂aj,i[l]∂u6=∂aj,i[l]∂u7∂u7∂u6=u4=exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l]),∂aj,i[l]∂u5=∂aj,i[l]∂u6∂u6∂u5=−u41u52=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u4=∂aj,i[l]∂u7∂u7∂u4=u6=1exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]),∂aj,i[l]∂u3=∂aj,i[l]∂u4∂u4∂u3+∂aj,i[l]∂u5∂u5∂u3=−u6−u41u52=−1exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])−exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=−2exp⁡(zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u2=∂aj,i[l]∂u4∂u4∂u2+∂aj,i[l]∂u5∂u5∂u2=u6−u41u52=1exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])−exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=2exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u1=∂aj,i[l]∂u3∂u3∂u1=(−u6−u41u52)exp⁡(u1)=−2exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u0=∂aj,i[l]∂u1∂u1∂u0+∂aj,i[l]∂u2∂u2∂u0=−(−u6−u41u52)exp⁡(u1)+(u6−u41u52)exp⁡(u0)=2exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2+2exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=4exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2.

It follows that

∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=4exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=exp⁡(zj,i[l])2+2exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])2(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])2−2exp⁡(zj,i[l])exp⁡(−zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l])2(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=1−(exp⁡(zj,i[l])−exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2(exp⁡(zj,i[l])+exp⁡(−zj,i[l]))2=1−aj,i[l]aj,i[l].

Similiar to the sigmoid activation function, we also have

∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=0,∀p≠j.

Thus,

∂J∂zj,i[l]=∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]+∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l]aj,i[l]),

which implies that

(6)∂J∂Z[l]=∂J∂A[l]⊙(1−A[l]⊙A[l]).

Softmax

The softmax activation function is given by

aj,i[l]=gj[l](z1,i[l],…,zj,i[l],…,zn[l],i[l])=exp⁡(zj,i[l])∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]).

Vectorization results in

(7)A[l]=1broadcast(∑axis=0exp⁡(Z[l])⏟row vector)⊙exp⁡(Z[l]).

To begin with, we construct a computation graph for the jth activation of the current layer:

u−1=zj,i[l],u0,p=zp,i[l],∀p≠j,u1=exp⁡(u−1),u2,p=exp⁡(u0,p),∀p≠j,u3=u1+∑p≠ju2,p,u4=1u3,u5=u1u4=aj,i[l].

By applying the chain rule, we get

∂aj,i[l]∂u5=1,∂aj,i[l]∂u4=∂aj,i[l]∂u5∂u5∂u4=u1=exp⁡(zj,i[l]),∂aj,i[l]∂u3=∂aj,i[l]∂u4∂u4∂u3=−u11u32=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2,∂aj,i[l]∂u1=∂aj,i[l]∂u3∂u3∂u1+∂aj,i[l]∂u5∂u5∂u1=−u11u32+u4=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2+1∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]),∂aj,i[l]∂u−1=∂aj,i[l]∂u1∂u1∂u−1=(−u11u32+u4)exp⁡(u−1)=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])2(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2+exp⁡(zj,i[l])∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]).

Next, we need to take into account that zj,i[l] also affects other activations in the same layer:

u−1=zj,i[l],u0,p=zp,i[l],∀p≠j,u1=exp⁡(u−1),u2,p=exp⁡(u0,p),∀p≠j,u3=u1+∑p≠ju2,p,u4=1u3,u5=u2,pu4=ap,i[l],∀p≠j.

Backward propagation gives us the remaining partial derivatives:

∂ap,i[l]∂u5=1,∂ap,i[l]∂u4=∂ap,i[l]∂u5∂u5∂u4=u2,p=exp⁡(zp,i[l]),∂ap,i[l]∂u3=∂ap,i[l]∂u4∂u4∂u3=−u2,p1u32=−exp⁡(zp,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2,∂ap,i[l]∂u1=∂ap,i[l]∂u3∂u3∂u1=−u2,p1u32=−exp⁡(zp,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2,∂ap,i[l]∂u−1=∂ap,i[l]∂u1∂u1∂u−1=−u2,p1u32exp⁡(u−1)=−exp⁡(zp,i[l])exp⁡(zj,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2.

We now know that

∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=−exp⁡(zj,i[l])2(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2+exp⁡(zj,i[l])∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l])=aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l]),∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=−exp⁡(zp,i[l])exp⁡(zj,i[l])(∑pexp⁡(zp,i[l]))2=−ap,i[l]aj,i[l],∀p≠j.

Hence,

∂J∂zj,i[l]=∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]∂aj,i[l]∂zj,i[l]+∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]∂ap,i[l]∂zj,i[l]=∂J∂aj,i[l]aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l])−∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]ap,i[l]aj,i[l]=aj,i[l](∂J∂aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l])−∑p≠j∂J∂ap,i[l]ap,i[l])=aj,i[l](∂J∂aj,i[l](1−aj,i[l])−∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]ap,i[l]+∂J∂aj,i[l]aj,i[l])=aj,i[l](∂J∂aj,i[l]−∑p∂J∂ap,i[l]ap,i[l]),

which we can vectorize as

∂J∂z:,i[l]=a:,i[l]⊙(∂J∂a:,i[l]−a:,i[l]⊺∂J∂a:,i[l]⏟scalar).

Let us not stop with the vectorization just yet:

(8)∂J∂Z[l]=A[l]⊙(∂J∂A[l]−broadcast(∑axis=0∂J∂A[l]⊙A[l]⏟row vector)).
